Unknown Panel Guide

Use when you can't identify the system

Step 1 — Identify the Panel
1
Look for a nameplate inside the cabinet door — brand and model are almost always there
2
Check the monitoring company sticker on the door — they can walk you through by phone
3
Look for a key switch — insert and turn before pressing anything
Step 2 — Silence the Alarm
1
Find the most prominent button labeled SILENCE, ACK, or showing a bell
2
Older panels: look for a physical toggle switch labeled SILENCE
3
ACKNOWLEDGE silences the local panel buzzer only — then press SILENCE for building-wide
4
Nothing responds: do not experiment — call the monitoring company
Step 3 — Read the Zone
1
LCD display: zone number and device type shown — write it down
2
LED panel: red light = that zone in alarm — find the zone directory card inside door
3
No display: conventional panel — each circuit = one zone, check wiring labels
Step 4 — Investigate and Reset
1
Investigate fully — do not reset until source is confirmed and corrected
2
Manual pull stations require physical reset at the device before panel clears
3
Press RESET — if re-alarm occurs, something is still active
4
Panel will not reset: leave silenced, document zone, notify monitoring company
What to Look For on Any Panel
RED LED
Active alarm in that zone
AMBER LED
Trouble / fault condition
GREEN LED
Normal / power on / all clear
BLUE LED
Supervisory / monitor point
KEY SWITCH
Insert and turn before anything
DOOR STICKER
Monitoring company number
NEVERDo NOT guess at unfamiliar panel functions. Silence, document the zone, and call monitoring when uncertain.
PRO TIPMonitoring company number is on the panel door sticker. They know the system and can confirm the zone.
